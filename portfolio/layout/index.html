Overview

With the rise in its popularity, the online gaming culture undeniably cultivates a sense of global community and connectivity, providing individuals with opportunities to forge friendships, collaborate on strategic endeavors, and engage in diverse cultural exchanges, thus enriching social interactions and broadening perspectives. However, this surge in participation has brought attention to toxic gaming culture, characterized by harassment, hate speech, and cyberbullying. Amid the expansive realm of online gaming, all players are subject to mainstream toxicity, yet female players find themselves as primary targets, frequently facing discrimination and derogatory treatment. With a particular focus on the world-renowned game, League of Legends, this discussion strives to unravel the distinct manifestations of toxic gaming culture, analyze the prevalent forms of harassment and discrimination directed at female gamers, and investigate the underlying factors within the game's design and the gaming industry that contribute to these issues.

As the online gaming community offers a diverse space for entertainment and social interaction, it is crucial to understand the dynamics of toxic gaming culture and its origins. Toxic gaming culture refers to the presence of harmful behaviors within online gaming communities, including but not limited to harassment, hate speech, and cyberbullying. Female gamers, in particular, often find themselves the primary targets of harassment and discrimination, experiencing consequences that extend beyond the boundaries of mere competition or banter, leading to severe mental health issues. This issue is not confined to a single game; rather, it is symptomatic of a broader concern embedded in the dynamics of various online gaming platforms. As the gaming community continues to grow in popularity, understanding the nuanced dynamics of toxic behavior becomes imperative for creating an environment that is both diverse and welcoming, ultimately transforming the landscape of online gaming into a space where everyone can participate without fear of discrimination or harassment.

What is League of Legends?

Analyzing the roots of toxic gaming culture leads the focus to Riot Games’ League of Legends, a game commonly known for both its widespread appeal and its particularly hostile community. Launched in 2009, League of Legends (LoL) quickly surged to the forefront of the gaming world, captivating millions of players globally with its strategic gameplay and fantasy-themed two-team battles. As a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game, LoL requires teamwork and tactical thinking as players assume various roles and champions with unique abilities, battling it out in intense, fast-paced matches varying from different maps and game modes. In a typical match (Summoner’s Rift Map), the primary objective is to collaborate in a 5-player team to destroy the opposing team's Nexus located in their base, thereby achieving victory by strategically outmaneuvering and overpowering the enemy. The game's popularity is evidenced by the 180 million monthly active players in 2022 (Samanta 2023), along with its annual esports tournament, League of Legends World Championship, representing the culmination of each season. However, LoL’s success does not seclude it from the inherent issues in online gaming culture. The game's competitive nature, combined with its large and diverse player base, has become a breeding ground for toxic behaviors, influencing the overall experience for many players, particularly targeting female participants within the virtual arenas of LoL. Investigating the dynamics of toxicity within LoL’s interface provides insights into expansive issues present in the gaming industry as a whole.
